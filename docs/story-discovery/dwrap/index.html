DWRAP: Who Hosts the World's Refugees?

Asylum Policy, 1951–2022

Data: UNHCR Refugee Data Finder; Dataset of World Refugee and Asylum Policies (Blair, Grossman & Weinstein / World Bank, 2024)

Where Does Displacement Begin — and End?

Every year, millions of people are forced from their homes by conflict, persecution, and climate-driven disaster. But the countries that generate displacement and the countries that absorb it are rarely the same — and the gap between them has grown wider over seven decades of international refugee law.

The Sankey above traces the dominant flows of the modern refugee era: from Afghanistan, Syria, and South Sudan toward neighbors that are themselves low- and middle-income countries. The pattern challenges the common assumption that resettlement in wealthy Western nations is the primary outcome.

The Global Hosting Burden

Mapping hosting rates per capita — refugees relative to host-country population — reveals a world almost inverted from public perception. Examine where the weight actually lands.

Policy Liberality — North vs. South

The DWRAP dataset scores national asylum policies across dozens of dimensions, from legal recognition standards to work authorization. Comparing those scores between the Global North and Global South exposes a striking asymmetry: the countries bearing the most people often offer the narrowest legal protections.
